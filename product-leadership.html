Product Leadership Beyond APIs

How I drove adoption, change, and value inside enterprise firms

Before I led platforms and APIs, I led something harder: people, workflows, and organizations that had to change how they worked.

As part of Thomson Reuters’ Professional Services and Training organizations, I implemented GoFileRoom inside top-100 tax and accounting firms across the United States. These were not greenfield SaaS startups — they were established, process-heavy organizations with real risk, real deadlines, and little tolerance for disruption.

The Reality on the Ground

These firms didn’t fail because of bad software. They struggled because:

  • People were attached to old processes
  • Partners didn’t trust new workflows
  • Staff feared losing productivity during change
  • Leadership wanted ROI, not features

My job was to make the product work inside that reality.

What I Actually Did

I was not just training users. I was running product adoption as a business outcome.

  • Mapped existing firm workflows and redesigned them around the software
  • Identified friction points that blocked adoption and escalated them into product changes
  • Led in-person and virtual change management for staff, managers, and partners
  • Translated product features into financial and operational value for firm leadership
  • Worked with engineering and product teams to close gaps that blocked real-world use

The Result

These firms didn’t just install software — they changed how they worked.

  • Document workflows that once took days were reduced to minutes
  • Firms saved 30+ hours per week through process and software optimization
  • Adoption increased because the product fit how people actually worked

This experience shaped how I later ran GoFileRoom as a Product Manager: I never treated roadmaps as technical plans — I treated them as change plans.

How This Informed My Product Leadership

When I later owned the GoFileRoom roadmap, I brought this mindset with me:

  • Features must solve real workflow problems, not just technical ones
  • Roadmaps must be credible to customers, not just engineering
  • Adoption and trust matter as much as delivery
  • Every product decision creates organizational change for someone

This is how I was able to rebuild customer trust, raise pNPS, and grow GoFileRoom from $38M to $43M as a Product Manager.

What This Means for You

Whether I am working on APIs, platforms, or traditional SaaS products, I lead with the same principle:

Products succeed when people can actually use them — and trust that they are going somewhere.

That is how I run roadmaps, teams, and platforms.
