The Business Reality of GoFileRoom

GoFileRoom was never a weak product.

When I took ownership, it was already generating about $38M annually with very little investment from the company. Customers trusted the platform to hold their most critical documents — but they did not trust that their needs were being heard or that the product would continue to evolve.

Internally, some believed the product had no long-term future because it lacked modernization, integrations, and a clear roadmap. Externally, customers were loyal — but increasingly frustrated by slow change, limited integrations, and poor visibility into issues.

My job was not to “save” GoFileRoom. It was to turn a trusted but under-invested product into a platform the company could confidently invest in.

What Changed Under My Leadership

I ran GoFileRoom as a business-critical platform, not just a backlog.

  • Created a platform roadmap tied directly to revenue and retention
  • Built telemetry and dashboards to expose outages, risk, and adoption in real time
  • Established escalation paths so enterprise customers were no longer left in the dark
  • Worked directly with power users to validate priorities and rebuild confidence

These changes made GoFileRoom investable again — and the revenue followed.

The API & Platform Program

APIs were not a side project. They were a strategic unlock.

We built APIs that allowed customers to automate document workflows, integrate GoFileRoom into tax and accounting ecosystems, and replace manual work that previously required multiple staff members.

We also built APIs for internal teams who relied on GoFileRoom for their own customer-facing products, eliminating blockers and turning disconnected systems into a shared platform.

I owned the API roadmap, platform priorities, developer experience, and governance — while engineering built the services.

One enterprise customer told us that our APIs replaced the work of three full-time staff members.

GoFileRoom went from a quietly profitable but under-invested product to a platform the company actively funds, grows, and believes in.

Revenue grew from $38M to over $43M. Customer trust rebounded. And the product now has a clear future.
